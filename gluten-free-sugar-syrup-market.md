<p><p>The gluten-free sugar syrup market encompasses various types, including glucose syrup, maple syrup, and corn syrup. Glucose syrup, derived from hydrolyzing starch, serves as a sweetener and thickener in food products. Maple syrup, produced from the sap of maple trees, is popular for its distinctive flavor and natural sweetness. Corn syrup, made from cornstarch, is widely utilized in processed foods to enhance sweetness and moisture retention. Together, these syrups cater to diverse consumer preferences for gluten-free options.</p></p>
