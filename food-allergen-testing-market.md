<p><p>The food allergen testing market is primarily categorized into three types: Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR)-based methods, Immunoassay-based methods (including ELISA), and other techniques. PCR-based testing detects specific DNA sequences associated with allergens, offering high sensitivity and specificity. Immunoassay techniques, like ELISA, identify proteins from allergens using antibodies, providing rapid and quantitative results. Other methods may include mass spectrometry or chromatography, which can analyze complex food matrices for allergenic substances, enhancing overall food safety and compliance.</p></p>
